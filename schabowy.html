Polish Pork Schnitzel with addings

This authentic Polish pork cutlets recipe produces some really tender and juicy meat that you will love!

Schabowy is Polish-style pork chops that are made by pounding a piece of pork till tender, coating it with breadcrumbs and frying it till it’s deliciously crispy

Believe it or not, the sound of pounding the cutlets with a meat mallet is what you can still hear in the block of flats every weekend.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pork loin chops
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and cut into wedges
  • 2 cucumbers, thinly sliced
  • 1 fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 fresh dill, chopped
  • 1 spoon of sour cream

Directions:

  1. Season the pork loin chops with salt and pepper on both sides. Place the flour, beaten eggs, and breadcrumbs into separate shallow bowls or plates
  2. Dredge each pork chop in flour, shaking off any excess. Dip the floured pork chop into the beaten eggs, ensuring it is evenly coated. Coat the pork chop with breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the meat. Repeat with the remaining pork chops.
  3. Dredge each pork chop in flour, shaking off any excess. Dip the floured pork chop into the beaten eggs, ensuring it is evenly coated. Coat the pork chop with breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the meat. Repeat with the remaining pork chops.
  4. For the potatoes, preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Place the potato wedges on a baking sheet and drizzle with vegetable oil. Sprinkle with salt and toss to coat. Bake the potatoes for about 30-35 minutes until they are golden and crispy.
  5. In a bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers and sour cream. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Toss to combine.
  6. Garnish the cucumber with chopped fresh dill.
  7. Garnish the potatoes with chopped fresh parsley for added flavor.
